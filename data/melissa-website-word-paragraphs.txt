50: Selected Projects
51: Climate Adaptation Planning – El Salvador UNEP / Government of El Salvador
52: El Salvador’s first National Adaptation Plan, a national process designed to strengthen long-term climate resilience by defining the country’s strategic direction for adaptation. The NAP helps align institutions, sectors, and territories around shared priorities while strengthening the country’s capacity to plan, implement, and scale adaptation action across four key areas: governance and institutional coordination, evidence-based adaptation solutions, private sector engagement, and adaptation finance.
53: Climate Risk and Resilience Assessment – Guatemala City Resilient Cities Catalyst
54: Guatemala City Climate Risk & Resilience Assessment
55: A project-level climate resilience assessment for the Bethania-Sakerty Ecological Park and Productive Ravine Project in Guatemala City, developed to translate climate risk into actionable planning and design. Through hazard prioritization, exposure analysis, and risk profiling across project components and user groups, the assessment helped define targeted adaptation and risk management strategies to strengthen long-term resilience, reduce potential losses, and guide project implementation.
56: Cities Forward Initiative – Latin America & United States ICLEI USA
57: A flagship hemispheric partnership of the U.S. Department of State, ICLEI – Local Governments for Sustainability, Resilient Cities Catalyst, and the Institute of the Americas, Cities Forward brought together 12 U.S. cities and 12 cities across Latin America and the Caribbean to address critical urban challenges related to sustainability, equity, and resilience. The initiative combined peer exchange, technical cooperation, diplomatic engagement, and capacity building to support city-led project design, strengthen feasibility and implementation readiness, and expand pathways toward investment and financing.
